What is Piles Surgery (Haemorrhoidectomy)?

Piles are swollen veins in the lower rectum or anus that cause pain, itching or bleeding during bowel movements. When medication and lifestyle changes don’t help, Haemorrhoidectomy - a surgical removal of piles - is recommended for lasting relief.

At Vasavi Hospitals, our experienced surgeons perform Advanced Laser and Conventional Piles Surgery with utmost precision and care. These methods ensure minimal pain, less bleeding and faster recovery, helping you return to normal life comfortably.

If you notice bleeding, pain or swelling around the anal region, it’s important to consult our specialists early for accurate diagnosis and safe treatment.

Know the Signs. Choose the Right Care.

Many people delay piles treatment due to embarrassment or fear of surgery. At Vasavi Hospitals, we provide safe, compassionate and effective solutions that relieve discomfort and prevent recurrence.

Common Symptoms

  • Bleeding during bowel movements
  • Pain, itching or burning sensation in the anal area
  • Swelling or lump around the anus
  • Mucus discharge or discomfort while sitting

Surgery Options

At Vasavi Hospitals, Piles Surgery is performed using Laser or Conventional methods depending on your condition. Laser treatment offers quicker healing and minimal discomfort, while conventional surgery ensures complete removal in severe cases.

Robotic vs. Laparoscopic Surgery: What's the Difference?

While both are minimally invasive, the Da Vinci Xi Robotic System offers superior precision, leading to faster recovery and better patient outcomes compared to traditional laparoscopy.

Robotic Surgery (Da Vinci Xi)

  • 3D HD Vision: Surgeon sees the surgical field in high-definition, three-dimensional view for unparalleled depth perception.

  • Enhanced Dexterity: Robotic instruments have seven degrees of motion, mimicking the human wrist, which allows for finer and more precise suturing (stitching).

  • Superior Precision: Filters out natural hand tremors and allows for more complex, delicate repairs.

  • Reduced Pain & Scarring: The high precision results in less trauma to surrounding tissue.

Laparoscopic Surgery

  • 2D Vision: Surgeons view a flat, two-dimensional image, which can limit depth perception.

  • Limited Movement: Instruments have restricted movement (4 degrees of motion), making complex suturing more challenging.

  • Good Precision: Excellent compared to open surgery, but lacks the micro-movements of a robotic system.

  • Minimal Scarring: Also offers small incisions, but robotic methods often allow for even smaller, strategically placed cuts.

What to Expect During Piles Surgery (Haemorrhoidectomy)

Your Step-by-Step Care Journey

1

Consultation & Evaluation

Meet our colorectal specialists for a detailed assessment. We review your symptoms, perform a clinical examination and recommend the best treatment plan based on your condition

2

Surgery Day

The procedure is performed under local or spinal anesthesia. Using Laser or Conventional methods, the swollen veins are carefully removed or sealed to stop bleeding and pain. The surgery usually takes 30–60 minutes.

3

Post-Surgery Stay

Most patients stay in the hospital for 1 day. Our team provides dietary guidance, pain management and hygiene tips to ensure a smooth and comfortable recovery.

4

Recovery & Follow-Up

You can resume normal activities within 3–5 days, depending on the surgery type. Follow-up visits help track healing, manage any discomfort and ensure long-term relief from piles.
