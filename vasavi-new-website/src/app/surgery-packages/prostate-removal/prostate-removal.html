What is Prostate Removal (TURP) Surgery?

Prostate removal, known as TURP (Transurethral Resection of the Prostate), is a common surgery performed to treat Benign Prostatic Hyperplasia (BPH) - a condition where the prostate gland becomes enlarged and blocks urine flow.

During the procedure, a special instrument called a resectoscope is passed through the urethra to remove excess prostate tissue, improving urine flow and relieving symptoms. There are no external cuts or stitches, making it a safe and effective treatment for urinary problems caused by an enlarged prostate.

Common Symptoms

  • Frequent urge to urinate, especially at night
  • Weak or slow urine flow
  • Difficulty starting or stopping urination
  • Feeling that the bladder doesn’t empty completely
  • Discomfort or burning while urinating

Robotic vs. Laparoscopic Surgery: What's the Difference?

While both are minimally invasive, the Da Vinci Xi Robotic System offers superior precision, leading to faster recovery and better patient outcomes compared to traditional laparoscopy.

Robotic Surgery (Da Vinci Xi)

  • 3D HD Vision: Surgeon sees the surgical field in high-definition, three-dimensional view for unparalleled depth perception.

  • Enhanced Dexterity: Robotic instruments have seven degrees of motion, mimicking the human wrist, which allows for finer and more precise suturing (stitching).

  • Superior Precision: Filters out natural hand tremors and allows for more complex, delicate repairs.

  • Reduced Pain & Scarring: The high precision results in less trauma to surrounding tissue.

Laparoscopic Surgery

  • 2D Vision: Surgeons view a flat, two-dimensional image, which can limit depth perception.

  • Limited Movement: Instruments have restricted movement (4 degrees of motion), making complex suturing more challenging.

  • Good Precision: Excellent compared to open surgery, but lacks the micro-movements of a robotic system.

  • Minimal Scarring: Also offers small incisions, but robotic methods often allow for even smaller, strategically placed cuts.

What to Expect During Prostate Removal (TURP) Surgery

Your Step-by-Step Care Journey

1

Consultation & Evaluation

Meet our urology team for a complete evaluation, including urine flow tests, ultrasound and PSA levels. We explain the condition, procedure and recovery process in detail before proceeding.

2

Surgery Day

The surgery is performed under spinal or general anesthesia. Using a resectoscope inserted through the urethra, the enlarged prostate tissue is carefully removed. The procedure takes about 60–90 minutes and leaves no external scars.

3

Post-Surgery Stay

You’ll stay in the hospital for 2-3 days for observation. A catheter is placed temporarily to help drain urine while the area heals. Our team ensures your comfort and provides clear instructions for recovery.

4

Recovery & Follow-Up

Most patients resume normal routines within 2-3 weeks. Follow-up visits help monitor healing, check urine flow and ensure long-lasting comfort and relief.
