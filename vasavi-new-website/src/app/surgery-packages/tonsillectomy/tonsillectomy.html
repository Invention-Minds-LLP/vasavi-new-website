What is Tonsillectomy Surgery?

A Tonsillectomy is a surgical procedure to remove the tonsils, two small glands located at the back of the throat. It is usually recommended for people - especially children - who suffer from repeated throat infections, breathing difficulties or sleep issues caused by enlarged or infected tonsils.

At Vasavi Hospitals, our experienced ENT surgeons perform Tonsillectomy Surgery safely and precisely using modern surgical techniques that ensure minimal discomfort and faster recovery. The procedure helps improve breathing, reduce snoring and prevent frequent throat infections.

If you or your child experience frequent sore throats, difficulty swallowing or loud snoring, it’s time to consult our ENT specialists for accurate diagnosis and care.

Know the Signs. Choose the Right Care.

Chronic tonsil infections can affect breathing, sleep and overall well-being. At Vasavi Hospitals, we provide expert evaluation and safe surgical treatment to ensure long-lasting relief and better throat health.

Common Symptoms

  • Frequent sore throat or tonsil infections
  • Difficulty swallowing or persistent throat pain
  • Loud snoring or mouth breathing during sleep
  • Swollen tonsils or visible white patches in the throat
  • Bad breath or fever due to recurrent infection

Robotic vs. Laparoscopic Surgery: What's the Difference?

While both are minimally invasive, the Da Vinci Xi Robotic System offers superior precision, leading to faster recovery and better patient outcomes compared to traditional laparoscopy.

Robotic Surgery (Da Vinci Xi)

  • 3D HD Vision: Surgeon sees the surgical field in high-definition, three-dimensional view for unparalleled depth perception.

  • Enhanced Dexterity: Robotic instruments have seven degrees of motion, mimicking the human wrist, which allows for finer and more precise suturing (stitching).

  • Superior Precision: Filters out natural hand tremors and allows for more complex, delicate repairs.

  • Reduced Pain & Scarring: The high precision results in less trauma to surrounding tissue.

Laparoscopic Surgery

  • 2D Vision: Surgeons view a flat, two-dimensional image, which can limit depth perception.

  • Limited Movement: Instruments have restricted movement (4 degrees of motion), making complex suturing more challenging.

  • Good Precision: Excellent compared to open surgery, but lacks the micro-movements of a robotic system.

  • Minimal Scarring: Also offers small incisions, but robotic methods often allow for even smaller, strategically placed cuts.

Your Step-by-Step Care Journey

1

Consultation & Evaluation

Meet our ENT specialists to discuss symptoms, medical history and perform a throat examination. We explain the need for surgery, cost and recovery expectations clearly before proceeding.

2

Surgery Day

The procedure is done under general anesthesia and takes about 30–45 minutes. The tonsils are gently removed through the mouth — with no external cuts or visible scars.

3

Post-Surgery Stay

Most patients can go home the same day after a short observation period. Our doctors provide guidance on diet (soft foods), hydration and medications for pain relief and comfort.

4

Recovery & Follow-Up

Recovery usually takes 7–10 days. Patients may experience mild throat pain or discomfort initially, which gradually improves. Follow-up visits ensure complete healing and prevention of future infections.
