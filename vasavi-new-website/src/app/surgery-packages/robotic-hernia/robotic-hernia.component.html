What is Hernia?

A hernia occurs when an internal organ or tissue pushes through a weak spot in the abdominal wall, often causing a small lump or discomfort. It can develop due to strain, heavy lifting, or long-term pressure on the abdomen.

Robotic vs. Laparoscopic Hernia Surgery: What's the Difference?

While both are minimally invasive, the Da Vinci Xi Robotic System offers superior precision, leading to faster recovery and better patient outcomes compared to traditional laparoscopy.

Robotic Surgery (Da Vinci Xi)

  • 3D HD Vision: Surgeon sees the surgical field in high-definition, three-dimensional view for unparalleled depth perception.

  • Enhanced Dexterity: Robotic instruments have seven degrees of motion, mimicking the human wrist, which allows for finer and more precise suturing (stitching).

  • Superior Precision: Filters out natural hand tremors and allows for more complex, delicate repairs.

  • Reduced Pain & Scarring: The high precision results in less trauma to surrounding tissue.

Laparoscopic Surgery

  • 2D Vision: Surgeons view a flat, two-dimensional image, which can limit depth perception.

  • Limited Movement: Instruments have restricted movement (4 degrees of motion), making complex suturing more challenging.

  • Good Precision: Excellent compared to open surgery, but lacks the micro-movements of a robotic system.

  • Minimal Scarring: Also offers small incisions, but robotic methods often allow for even smaller, strategically placed cuts.
