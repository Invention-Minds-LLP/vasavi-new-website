What is Gallbladder Stone Removal Surgery?

Gallbladder removal, also known as Cholecystectomy, is a surgical procedure performed to remove the gallbladder when it contains stones or causes pain, infection or digestive problems. Gallstones are hardened deposits that block bile flow, leading to severe abdominal discomfort, nausea and inflammation.

At Vasavi Hospitals, our experienced surgeons perform both Laparoscopic and Robotic Gallbladder Removal procedures for safer, quicker and more precise results. These advanced methods help reduce pain, minimize scars and promote faster recovery.

If you experience sharp pain in the upper right abdomen, nausea after meals or indigestion, meet our specialists for an accurate diagnosis and timely treatment.

Robotic vs. Laparoscopic Surgery: What's the Difference?

While both are minimally invasive, the Da Vinci Xi Robotic System offers superior precision, leading to faster recovery and better patient outcomes compared to traditional laparoscopy.

Robotic Surgery (Da Vinci Xi)

  • 3D HD Vision: Surgeon sees the surgical field in high-definition, three-dimensional view for unparalleled depth perception.

  • Enhanced Dexterity: Robotic instruments have seven degrees of motion, mimicking the human wrist, which allows for finer and more precise suturing (stitching).

  • Superior Precision: Filters out natural hand tremors and allows for more complex, delicate repairs.

  • Reduced Pain & Scarring: The high precision results in less trauma to surrounding tissue.

Laparoscopic Surgery

  • 2D Vision: Surgeons view a flat, two-dimensional image, which can limit depth perception.

  • Limited Movement: Instruments have restricted movement (4 degrees of motion), making complex suturing more challenging.

  • Good Precision: Excellent compared to open surgery, but lacks the micro-movements of a robotic system.

  • Minimal Scarring: Also offers small incisions, but robotic methods often allow for even smaller, strategically placed cuts.

What to Expect During Gallbladder Stone Removal Surgery

Your Step-by-Step Care Journey

1

Consultation & Evaluation

Our surgical team will assess your symptoms, perform scans like ultrasound or CT and confirm the presence of gallstones. We explain the procedure, expected recovery and cost clearly before surgery.

2

Surgery Day

The procedure is done under general anesthesia using Laparoscopic or Robotic-assisted methods. Small incisions are made to remove the gallbladder safely. The surgery usually takes about 1 hour, ensuring minimal blood loss and quicker healing.

3

Post-Surgery Stay

You’ll stay in the hospital for 1–2 days under observation. Our team monitors your recovery and provides diet guidance and care instructions to avoid post-surgery discomfort.

4

Recovery & Follow-Up

Most patients resume normal activities within 5–7 days. Follow-up visits ensure proper healing, dietary balance and digestive comfort after surgery..
