What is Fistula Surgery?

An Fistula is an abnormal tunnel that forms between the anal canal and the skin near the anus, often caused by an infection or abscess. It leads to recurring pain, swelling and discharge, and rarely heals on its own - making surgery the most effective treatment.

At Vasavi Hospitals, our surgeons perform Fistulectomy or Fistulotomy, depending on the complexity of the fistula. Both procedures are designed to remove or open the infected tract safely, ensuring minimal discomfort and faster recovery.

If you experience persistent pain, pus discharge or swelling around the anal area, consult our specialists early for the right diagnosis and treatment.

Know the Signs. Choose the Right Care.

Ignoring a fistula can lead to repeated infections and discomfort. At Vasavi Hospitals, we focus on accurate diagnosis and advanced surgical care to help patients recover completely and prevent recurrence.

Common Symptoms

  • Recurrent pain or swelling near the anus
  • Pus or blood discharge from an opening near the anal area
  • Itching, irritation or discomfort while sitting
  • Repeated abscess formation

Surgery Options

At Vasavi Hospitals, Fistula Surgery is performed using Fistulectomy or Fistulotomy techniques, depending on the depth and location of the tract. Our surgeons ensure minimal tissue damage, effective healing and reduced chances of recurrence.

What to Expect During Fistula Surgery

Your Step-by-Step Care Journey

1

Consultation & Evaluation

Meet our surgical team for a detailed assessment. We review your symptoms and may recommend diagnostic scans to locate the fistula tract precisely before surgery.

2

Surgery Day

The surgery is performed under spinal or general anesthesia. The fistula tract is either removed (Fistulectomy) or opened and cleaned (Fistulotomy) to allow it to heal naturally from the inside out. The procedure typically lasts 45-90 minutes.

3

Post-Surgery Stay

Most patients stay in the hospital for 1-2 days for observation. Our team provides wound care instructions, dietary advice, and medications to ensure smooth healing.

4

Recovery & Follow-Up

You can resume light activities within 3-5 days. Follow-up visits are important to monitor healing, prevent recurrence, and ensure long-term comfort and recovery.
