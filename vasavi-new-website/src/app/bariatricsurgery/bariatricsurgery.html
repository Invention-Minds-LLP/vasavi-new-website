Our Bariatric & Metabolic Treatments

Gastric Bypass Surgery

A well-established weight loss surgery that reduces stomach size and reroutes part of the digestive tract. This helps limit food intake, reduce calorie absorption, and manage conditions like type 2 diabetes and hypertension

Sleeve Gastrectomy

Involves removing approximately 70–80% of the stomach, leaving a smaller, sleeve-shaped stomach. This reduces hunger hormones and helps you feel full sooner.

Adjustable Gastric Banding

A silicone band is placed around the upper portion of the stomach, creating a smaller pouch to restrict food intake.

Revisional Bariatric Surgery

For patients who have had previous bariatric surgery but are experiencing complications or insufficient weight loss. We offer safe, personalized revision options based on your current health status.

Metabolic Surgery

Specially designed to treat metabolic disorders such as type 2 diabetes, high cholesterol, and insulin resistance—even in patients who are not severely obese.

Weight Management Programs

FAQs

1. What is Bariatric Surgery?
Bariatric surgery is a medical procedure that helps people lose weight by changing the stomach’s size or the way food is digested. It’s recommended for those who are unable to lose weight through diet and exercise alone.
2. Who is a good candidate for Bariatric Surgery?
A good candidate for bariatric surgery is someone with a BMI of 35 or more, or a BMI of 30+ along with health conditions such as diabetes, high blood pressure, or sleep apnea. Doctors carefully evaluate each individual’s health to determine if surgery is the right option.
3. What are the common types of Bariatric Surgery?
The most common types include Gastric Sleeve, Gastric Bypass, Duodenal Switch and Gastric Balloon. Each has different benefits, and our team will help you choose the best option.
4. Is Bariatric Surgery Safe?
Yes, when performed by experienced surgeons like ours, bariatric surgery is considered safe. We use advanced laparoscopic and robotic techniques for minimal pain and faster recovery.
5. How much weight can I expect to lose?
Most patients lose 50-70% of excess body weight within the first 12-18 months after surgery, depending on the procedure and lifestyle changes.
6. Will I need to follow a special diet after surgery?
Yes, our nutritionists will guide you through a step-by-step diet plan after surgery to ensure proper healing and healthy weight loss.
7. How long will it take to recover?
Recovery usually takes about 2-4 weeks, depending on the type of surgery. Minimally invasive procedures like laparoscopic and robotic surgery allow for a quicker return to normal activities.
8. Can Bariatric Surgery help with diabetes?
Yes! Bariatric surgery is also called metabolic surgery because it helps improve or even resolve Type 2 Diabetes in many patients.
9. What if I regain weight after surgery?
Our weight management programs and follow-up care are designed to keep you on track. In some cases, revisional bariatric surgery can help restore weight loss results.
